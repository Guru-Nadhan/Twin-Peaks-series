Twin Peaks is an American mystery-horror serial drama television series created by Mark Frost and David Lynch. It premiered on ABC on April 8, 1990, and ran for two seasons until its cancellation in 1991. In the years following its release, the show gained a devoted cult following and has been referenced in a wide variety of media. Twin Peaks is often listed among the greatest television series of all time, and has received universal acclaim from critics and audiences. It is considered a landmark turning point in television drama.

The series follows an investigation, headed by FBI Special Agent Dale Cooper (Kyle MacLachlan) and local Sheriff Harry S. Truman, into the murder of homecoming queen Laura Palmer (Sheryl Lee) in the fictional town of Twin Peaks, Washington. The show's narrative draws on elements of detective fiction, but its uncanny tone, supernatural elements, and campy, melodramatic portrayal of eccentric characters also pays homage to American soap opera and horror tropes. Like much of Lynch's work, it is distinguished by surrealism, offbeat humor, and distinctive cinematography. The score was composed by Angelo Badalamenti with Lynch.

Lead Characters

Dale Cooper

The main Protagonist of the series. He is an FBI agent, assigned to investigate the murder of Laura Palmer.

Laura Palmer

The homecoming queen of Twin Peaks highschool, who was well known and loved but was mysteriously murdered.

Sheriff Truman

The Sheriff in charge of the town, known for his sincerity and dedication to his duty. Partner of Dale Cooper.

Audrey Horne

She is the daughter of wealthy businessman Benjamin Horne, who often neglected her in favor of Laura Palmer.

Donna Hayward

She was the best friend of Laura Palmer, and after her death she was obsessed with finding out who killed her and why.

Shelly Jhonson

A charming married woman who works as a waitress at the RR diner,who is also in love with Bobby Briggs

Norma Jennings

The caring and sweet owner of the RR diner who serves the best coffee. She is also the best friend of Shelly Jhonson.

Bobby Briggs

The love interest of Shelly Jhonson, he is a mischevious teenager, who was once involved with Laura Palmer.
